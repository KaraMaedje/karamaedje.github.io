job description



My job at Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada was as an Agri-Environmental Data Analysis Assistant. I had the chance to work hands on with soil, nutrient and water management scientists and engineers with their on-going projects that contribute to the development of innovative solutions for water and soil conservation at a farm level. These projects work towards making landscape-scale assessments of agricultural sustainability and most importantly on the subject of nutrient loss for the Great Lakes. My exact job was to assist with the current phase of an in-progress project based on Temporal and Spatial Analysis of Agriculture Production Systems in Ontario and the Lake Erie basin. The project relied on a custom Typology ruleset created at AAFC. With the project already being in process and quite a few phases into development, it became my job to manage the project and develop innovative solutions towards completing it. With the job role being very self-disciplined, the early stages required taking a lot of time familiarizing myself with the data structure and hierarchy. I researched and discussed with my supervisors on their ideal image for the end goal of the project, in other words how we wanted this data to be transformed into something meaningful. I helped propose visual concepts and prototypes for what this project could transform into. Once a feasible end goal was decided upon, it became my job to consolidate, clean and format all the data we had, also while keeping track of anything missing data and the sources. Once this step was completed I moved onto the next phase which was handling suppressed values in all the data files. This is when the majority of the coding came into play. There was some trial and error between choosing which language to work in, changing from SQL to Python. I had to develop a procedure that would be able to parse individual masses of data and implement our custom Typology ruleset. This phase took a considerable about of time and was the final stage I was able to reach during my 4 month period. I was able to finish this suppression procedure for all the Ontario files for census years of 1981-2016, allowing them to be ready for QA.
