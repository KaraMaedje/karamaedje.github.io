job description



My job at Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada was as an Agri-Environmental Data Analysis Assistant. I had the chance to work hands on with soil, nutrient and water management scientists and engineers with their on-going projects that contribute to the development of innovative solutions for water and soil conservation at a farm level. These projects work towards making landscape-scale assessments of agricultural sustainability and most importantly on the subject of nutrient loss for the Great Lakes. My exact job was to assist with the current phase of an in-progress project based on Temporal and Spatial Analysis of Agriculture Production Systems in Ontario and the Lake Erie basin. The project relied on a custom Typology ruleset created at AAFC. With the project already being in process and quite a few phases into development, it became my job to manage the project and develop innovative solutions towards completing it. Last summer during my third co-op term, I had been the first student to begin working on this project in it's current phase, during the fall, another student took over from where I had left off and upon my return I continued where he left off. The project this time around was in more of a quality check and revise and editing phase. It became my job to go over all the scripts and make sure they were working as needed to be. This meant understanding all the suppression method implementations and testing their outputs on the data. I had to make some changes to the scripts and then book meetings to make sure the whole team agreed on the outcomes. Most of my term this time was spent working on perfecting the suppression estimation implementation and correcting the outputs so that the data could finally be used towards the last stages of the project, visualization.
